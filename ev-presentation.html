👷 Workforce Dynamics

Who Builds the Network?

EV deployment requires specialized labor — and both cities face different workforce realities.

🇩🇪

Berlin: Ecosystem Depth

• EUREF as live training ground — Audi engineers, energy researchers on-site

• Dual education system (Ausbildung) produces certified electricians at scale

• Ubitricity, Compleo provide specialized installation workforces

• CityLAB cultivates data science + urban planning talent

🇨🇿

Prague: Emerging Capacity

• Škoda Enyaq iV production → growing EV manufacturing workforce

• But: charger installation ≠ vehicle manufacturing

• Limited specialized EV infrastructure training programs

• Heritage installation requires additional skills (historic masonry, concealment)

🔧

Key gap: Prague has auto manufacturing workforce (Škoda), but building cars ≠ installing chargers. Heritage-sensitive installation is an entirely different skill set that doesn't exist at scale.

🇩🇪 Social Factors · Berlin

E-Bike Culture, EV-Curious

🚲

Micro-Mobility Adoption

E-bikes ubiquitous. East Side Gallery: dense e-bike parking alongside tourists. Berlin has culturally embraced electric personal mobility.

🚇

Transit Paradox

Exceptional public transit (U-Bahn, S-Bahn, trams) + €49/month Deutschlandticket reduces urgency for private EV ownership.

🚗

Demand-Side Gap

Despite 70K EVs, Uber/delivery drivers at EUREF showed zero interest — margins too tight. Infrastructure outpacing professional demand.

"The infrastructure is scaling, but demand-side drivers remain uneven — particularly among margin-driven ride-hail and delivery workers."

— EUREF-Campus observation

🎯 Strategic Insights

Three Core Findings

1. Infrastructure ≠ Adoption

Berlin proves that building chargers doesn't automatically drive EV adoption. Uber drivers at EUREF showed zero interest despite world-class infrastructure meters away. Supply without demand creates expensive ghost infrastructure.

2. Constraint Breeds Innovation

Prague's lamppost strategy emerged because conventional deployment is blocked. This constraint-driven innovation could leapfrog station-based models. The 6,000 lamppost target is Prague's most viable path.

3. The Gap Is Governance, Not Geography

The divide isn't wealth or technology — it's institutional culture. Berlin experiments and iterates. Prague restricts and studies. Changing institutional culture is harder than installing chargers.

💡 Recommendations

Strategic Implications

For Berlin

Close the Demand Gap

Fleet electrification mandates for ride-hail/delivery. TCO education for gig workers. Address East/West deployment imbalance.

For Prague

Accelerate Lampposts

Double down on lamppost conversion — only heritage-compatible path at scale. Mandate interoperability across PRE/EON/CEZ. Create a Prague CityLAB equivalent.

For Prague

Leverage Škoda's Transition

Partner with Škoda Enyaq iV marketing. Use national pride as adoption vector: "Drive Czech, drive electric." Bundle charger installation with purchases.

For Both

AFIR as Floor, Not Ceiling

Exceed EU minimums to build consumer confidence. Berlin: use AFIR for East/West gaps. Prague: use deadlines to overcome institutional inertia.
